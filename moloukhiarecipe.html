What is Kafta?

Kafta is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from ground meat (typically beef, lamb, or a mix of both), blended with onions, parsley, and a medley of spices. The mixture is shaped into skewers or patties and then grilled, baked, or pan-fried. Kafta is a staple in many Middle Eastern cuisines and is often served with rice, flatbread, or fresh salads.

Ingredients

  • 500g Molokhia (Jute leaves) – fresh or frozen
  • 1 large onion – finely chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic – minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 chicken or vegetable stock cube
  • 1.5 liters chicken broth – or water
  • Salt and pepper – to taste
  • Optional: 1 tbsp lemon juice for tanginess

The Recipe

  1. Prepare the Mixture: Combine ground meat, grated onion, parsley, and spices in a bowl. Mix well with your hands until the mixture is well combined.
  2. Shape the Kafta: Divide the mixture into equal portions. Shape into oblong patties or mold onto skewers for grilling.
  3. Cook:
    • Grill: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Cook the skewers for 3-4 minutes on each side until cooked through.
    • Bake: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Arrange kafta on a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, turning halfway.
    • Pan-Fry: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook kafta patties for 3-4 minutes on each side.
  4. Serve: Serve warm with rice, tahini sauce, hummus, or a fresh salad.
